Season's hottest day

Most of India may suffer from above normal day time and night time temperatures along with heatwaves in the summer season of April-May-June, according to the latest seasonal outlook issued by the India Meteorological Department (IMD).Temperatures across India are already soaring, with four states having recorded either heatwave conditions or warm night conditions between March 27 and April 1. These are Madhya Pradesh, Maharashtra, Rajasthan and Chhattisgarh.Heatwave conditions prevail when the maximum temperatures of a place cross 40 degrees Celsius (°C) for the plains and are above normal by 4.5°C. For coastal areas, the threshold is 37°C and for the hills, it is 30°C.IMD says that except for isolated places in east, the Northeast and northwest, the rest of India may likely experience above normal maximum temperatures in April, May and June. The prediction is similar for the month of April as well.People may not get much respite from the heat even during the nights, as the weather agency forecasts above normal minimum temperatures for all of India barring a few areas in the Northeast and northwest of the country. The prediction remains the same for the month of April as well. IMD also predicted that above normal heatwave days could occur in most parts of the southern peninsula, central India, east India and the plains of northwest India. The states with the highest anomaly of days with heatwaves during April-May-June are Gujarat, Maharashtra, Rajasthan, Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, Madhya Pradesh and Andhra Pradesh. The warning for hot temperatures and heatwaves comes at a time when the El Nino event in the equatorial Pacific Ocean is in its waning phase. IMD predicted that the El Nino — generally responsible for warmer temperatures in many places around the world including India may turn neutral within the season, with a possibility of La Nina conditions developing during the monsoon season.

With 48 Lok Sabha seats, Maharashtra is has the second-highest number of seats after Uttar Pradesh, which has 80 seats, making it a key state to all political parties. Maharashtra is a difficult state for parties hoping to win big in elections because of its unpredictable electoral process. The state has significant influence over decisions made at crucial points in time because of its representation in the Lok Sabha.There are 48 parliamentary constituencies in the state. Of these, four are set aside for ST candidates and five are designated for SC candidates. There are 39 seats left unclaimed.In the 2024 Lok Sabha elections, the Shiv Sena (UBT) will field candidates for 21 seats, the Congress for 17 seats, and the NCP (SP) for the remaining 10, in accordance with the seat-sharing formula of the opposition Maha Vikas Aghadi (MVA) in Maharashtra. The ruling Mahayuti - BJP, Shinde faction of Shiv Sena and Ajit Pawar NCP have not yet announced the formula. The BJP won 23 of the 25 seats in the 2019 Lok Sabha elections, while the Shiv Sena as a whole won 18 of the 23 seats.

Maharashtra Loksabha Election 2024

As part of the opposition alliance, the NCP remained united and ran for 19 seats, winning four of them.The fierce competition for the Lok Sabha in Maharashtra is evident from the key candidates from different parties contesting this time. The BJP has proposed two Union ministers: Piyush Goyal from Mumbai North and Nitin Gadkari from Nagpur, an RSS stronghold. The daughter of former chief minister Sharad Pawar, Supriya Sule, is prepared to carry on the family tradition by contesting for the Baramati Lok Sabha seat. There will be seven voting phases for the general elections in 2024, starting on April 19 and ending on June 1. Voting in Maharashtra will take place on April 19, April 26, May 7, May 13, and May 20 - in five phases. The fifth phase of the election will see polls taken in each of Mumbai's six parliamentary constituencies. June 4 is the date of the vote count.
